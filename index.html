How Lastly watches over your message.

Thorough by design. Your message passes through every possible checkpoint — and only arrives when there's no question left that it should.

What witnesses can do

Your witnesses never see your message. They only receive a request asking whether you may have passed away. Their role is to slow the process down, not speed it up.

The rule that never changes

At every stage before delivery, one tap from you stops the process. That is true during regular check-ins, during the extra direct attempts, and during the final 72-hour warning window.

If you respondEverything resets, the alarm is cleared, and the next scheduled check-in is set as normal.
If no one can confirmLastly resets the clock and starts the cycle over one more time — it checks in with the user again, escalates to witnesses again, and if witnesses are still unsure or their responses conflict, the messages are kept safe and encrypted indefinitely unless a witness, an emergency contact, or our support team provides verified confirmation.
Only one irreversible moment existsOnce the 72-hour window ends without a response and delivery begins, the process is complete and can no longer be undone.

Silence alone will never deliver your messages.
Lastly is built to be certain, not fast.
