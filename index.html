The Guardian System

How Lastly watches over your message.

This system is designed to be cautious, human-led, and easy to stop. Silence by itself never causes delivery. Real people must confirm what happened, and you can still cancel before the final step.

Guardian overview
L.A.S.T ECHO FLOW

A plain-language map of how check-ins, escalation, witnesses, emergency contact, and final delivery work together. The main path runs down the center. Safe exits loop away in green. Non-response and irreversible paths appear in red.

Stage 1

Regular check-in

Lastly checks in on the schedule you chose. You get a push notification, an email, and a text message.

If you respond, everything resets.
Stage 2

We try harder to reach you

If you miss the regular check-in, Lastly makes three more direct attempts on Day 1, Day 3, and Day 7.

If you respond at any point, everything resets.
Stage 3

Your witnesses are asked

On Day 21, your witnesses receive the same email and choose one of three answers.

  • Confirm → the 72-hour countdown begins
  • Dismiss → everything is cancelled
  • “I’m not sure” → your emergency contact is asked
Stage 3B

Emergency contact

If witnesses are uncertain, your emergency contact gets the same choice: confirm, dismiss, or “I’m not sure.”

Confirm starts the countdown. Dismiss cancels. “I’m not sure” cancels for safety.
Stage 4

Final 72-hour window

This is your last chance to stop everything. If you respond now, the countdown is cancelled and your witnesses are told it was a false alarm.

Stage 5

Delivery

If 72 hours pass with no response, your recipients receive their echoes. Once delivery begins, it cannot be reversed.

Safeguard

Lastly chooses caution

If two full escalation cycles end without a clear answer, the system becomes dormant. Your messages stay encrypted and preserved until verified manual review.

What witnesses can do

Your witnesses never see your message. They only receive a request asking whether you may have passed away. Their role is to slow the process down, not speed it up.

Countdown beginsBoth witnesses confirm. One confirms while the other stays silent. A single witness confirms. If you chose zero witnesses, Lastly skips this stage and moves to the 72-hour window as a best-effort fallback.
Everything is cancelledBoth dismiss. One confirms while the other dismisses. One dismisses while the other stays silent. A single witness dismisses. Dismissal always wins.
Emergency contact is askedBoth witnesses say “I'm not sure.” One witness says “I'm not sure.” Both stay silent. If there is no emergency contact in an uncertain case, Lastly chooses caution and cancels instead.

The rule that never changes

At every stage before delivery, one tap from you stops the process. That is true during regular check-ins, during the extra direct attempts, and during the final 72-hour warning window.

If you respondEverything resets, the alarm is cleared, and the next scheduled check-in is set as normal.
If no one can confirmLastly does not keep escalating forever. After two full unresolved cycles, the system becomes dormant and waits for verified manual review.
Only one irreversible moment existsOnce the 72-hour window ends without a response and delivery begins, the process is complete and can no longer be undone.

Silence alone will never deliver your messages.
Lastly is built to be certain, not fast.
